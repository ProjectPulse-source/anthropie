The two books of the anthropic framework form the long-form foundation of the project. Where the *Anthropie Working Papers* formalise the hypothesis and apply it to specific objects — public debt, the energy transition, independent research —, the books deploy it across a broader arc: long-range history, economics, public finance, epistemology. The founding book is available in English as *ANTHROPY – A Big History of Civilization's Hidden Costs*, whose subtitle carries the framework's motto: "Order here. Debt elsewhere." Every local order is financed by disorder displaced somewhere else, toward other places, other times, or other social groups. Each book can be read independently; together, they map the full architecture of the framework.

The other works belong to a separate editorial project, prior to or running alongside the anthropic framework: *L'Odyssée des idées*, a narrative journey through the history of ideas, and *Livresque des mots*, a quotation anthology in the lineage of the commonplace book — twenty-five centuries of voices, deliberately left unclassified, to be read like a novel rather than consulted like a dictionary. These three are published in French.
